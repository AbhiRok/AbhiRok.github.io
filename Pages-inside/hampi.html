Description

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site nestled in Karnataka, India, is a mesmerizing tapestry of ancient ruins and stunning landscapes. Once the majestic capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, Hampi now stands as a testament to its glorious past, with its sprawling temple complexes, intricate carvings, and imposing boulders. Explore the iconic Virupaksha Temple, witness the surreal sunset from Hemakuta Hill, and wander through the labyrinthine lanes of the vibrant Hampi Bazaar. Whether you're an architecture enthusiast, history buff, or nature lover, Hampi's timeless charm and mystical aura promise an unforgettable journey through time and culture.
